Phase 19: Reviewer and Maintenance Strategy Rollout
===================================================

Phase Goal
----------
Turn the already-landed reviewer and maintenance internals into controlled rollout families after phase-18 host stabilization, without widening the public surface or regressing retrieval-first and host-adapter guarantees.


Dependencies
------------
- Phase 17 retrieval-first closure complete
- Phase 18 host adapters accepted and closed so rollout-gate work does not mix with host-surface churn
- Phase 15/16 proposal and maintenance foundations remain stable under current regression suites


Primary Deliverables
--------------------
- an executable phase-19 task decomposition under `task-board/phase-19-reviewer-and-maintenance-strategy-rollout/`
- family-aware rollout substrate in `src/eval/*` that preserves retrieval behavior while allowing reviewer and maintenance families to plug in
- reviewer rollout candidate support with `observe -> assist -> promote`
- maintenance rollout candidate support with the same lifecycle
- family-specific shadow, gate, dashboard, and quality-gate artifacts
- evidence-backed public-surface re-evaluation that keeps advanced knobs internal unless phase-19 evidence justifies exposure


Definition of Done
------------------
- phase 19 task-board is detailed enough to execute slice-by-slice without re-planning
- reviewer strategies can run through `observe`, `assist`, and `promote` without changing unsupported defaults
- maintenance strategies can run through `observe`, `assist`, and `promote` without changing unsupported defaults
- promotion decisions are backed by family-specific eval evidence and persisted artifacts
- retrieval-first phase-17 guarantees and phase-18 host-adapter guarantees remain intact while later families land
- every completed subtask has a recorded targeted regression pass before the next subtask begins

Execution Order
---------------
1. Rewrite the phase-19 board and create subtask files.
2. Land shared rollout substrate in eval/runtime/reporting without changing retrieval semantics.
3. Land reviewer rollout family and its targeted regression slice.
4. Land maintenance rollout family and its targeted regression slice.
5. Add reviewer and maintenance quality-gate scripts plus release/doc checks.
6. Re-run public-surface classification with phase-19 evidence and keep unsupported knobs internal by default.


Candidate Families
------------------
- reviewer
  - baseline: `rules-only`
  - candidate: `assisted`
- maintenance
  - baseline: `default-hygiene`
  - candidate: `outcome-aware`


Per-Subtask Regression Rule
---------------------------
- Every subtask starts with failing tests for the new behavior.
- Every subtask ends with the targeted regression commands listed in its subtask file.
- Do not begin the next subtask until the current subtask regressions pass.


Key Constraints
---------------
- do not widen public runtime config by default
- do not regress retrieval-first rollout guarantees while adding new families
- reviewer and maintenance rollout evidence must remain distinguishable from provider-backed retrieval gains
- keep `goodmemory/evolution`, rollout config, promotion-gate runtime controls, and other advanced knobs internal unless phase-19 evidence explicitly changes that decision
- do not silently reuse retrieval strategy labels as reviewer or maintenance labels; family-specific rollout metadata must stay explicit
- `observe` mode must keep candidate behavior off the executed path through isolated shadow state
- high-risk maintenance and review mutations must continue to go through proposal/gate/compiler controls rather than direct default mutation
